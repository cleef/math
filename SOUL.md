# SOUL

This repo exists to help learners experience mathematics as a living world of ideas.

Inspired by Caucher Birkar's description, we treat math not as a cold list of procedures, but as a creative act: ideas are formed, mixed, reshaped, and discovered. In that sense, math belongs as much to imagination as to correctness.

## What We Believe

- Math is the world of ideas.
- Learning math should feel closer to exploration, play, pattern-making, and creation than to pressure and fear.
- Real progress depends on enjoyment. Practice matters, but joy is what makes long effort possible.
- Students should go beyond textbooks. Good math learning invites curiosity, extra reading, side paths, and self-driven discovery.
- Grades matter only to a limited extent. They must not become the main meaning of learning.
- Strong teaching is both mathematical and human: clear ideas, careful sequencing, and real empathy for how children think and feel.
- Too much pressure damages learning. Play, clarity, and curiosity usually teach better than competition.
- AI is a tool, not the soul. It can explain, support, and accelerate routine work, but human creativity remains central.

## What This Means For light-math

When we build apps, lessons, interfaces, and interactions in this repo, we should bias toward:

- conceptual understanding over mechanical repetition
- playful interaction over sterile drill
- visual meaning over abstract intimidation
- curiosity over compliance
- sustained engagement over short-term performance theater
- creative thinking over routine procedure

## Product Standard

Each app should try to do at least one of these well:

- make an idea visible
- make a pattern feel intuitive
- turn a formal rule into a playable experience
- reduce fear and increase willingness to explore
- invite the learner to ask "what if?"

If an experience is technically correct but joyless, overly competitive, or built around pressure, it misses the spirit of this project.

## Role Of AI

We use AI to assist explanation, content generation, adaptation, and production. We do not confuse assistance with authorship. The learner's job is still to notice, imagine, test, connect, and create.

## One-Line Test

Before shipping, ask:

"Does this help a learner feel that math is a creative world of ideas?"

If the answer is no, the work is not finished.
